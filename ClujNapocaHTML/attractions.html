What to do in Cluj-Napoca

Studies have shown that the city under Feleac hill has the biggest cultural vitality in Romania, subsequent to the capital Bucharest. Opens external link in new windowThe Transylvania International Film Festival (TIFF) has become a real brand pertaining to Cluj and has established itself as a landmark event in Central and Eastern Europe.

Poets say that Cluj shows its most beautiful face in autumn, when you can listen "the first white frost singing". So, it is by no accident that the country’s most enduring classical music festival, Opens external link in new window“Toamna muzicală clujeană” (Cluj Musical Autumn), which will be celebrating 50 years of existence in 2016, is held here every autumn. Opens external link in new windowInterferences Theatre Festival, Opens external link in new windowTemps d'Images Festival, Opens external link in new windowComedy Film Festival, Opens external link in new windowMozart Festival, Opens external link in new windowSonoro Chamber Music Festival, Spring of Cafes, Jazz in the Park, Photo Romania Festival, Lucian Blaga International Poetry Festival or Transylvania Jazz Festival are all just completing the image of Cluj, as a city of festivals.

But there is more. Electric Castle - an electronic music festival organised at Banffy castle in Bonțida village (40 kilometres from Cluj-Napoca) - became in the last three years one of the biggest festivals in Romania reaching an audience of 97,000 people from all around the world. Launched in 2015 as a highlight event of the European Youth Capital, Untold festival has reached an audience of 240,000 people only in four days (15% being foreigners) – thus winning the title of Europe’s Best Largest Festival. There is also a large number of traditional cultural events that generate the development of cultural tourism and contribute to the creation of a sense of belonging to a very diverse community: Cluj Days, Hungarian Cultural Days, art exhibitions at the contemporary art centre the Paintbrush Factory and many others.

Beside these, there are other more than 2,000 smaller or bigger events that complete the cultural dimension of the city.
